## Design Context

### Users
Indoor cyclists who train with BLE-compatible smart trainers (Tacx, etc.), heart rate monitors, and cadence/speed sensors. They use Bfree in a browser — often on a laptop or tablet propped up in front of their bike setup, sometimes in a dim room. They want to ride, see their data, and stay in control of their privacy. The job: start a ride quickly, track performance metrics in real time, and review results afterward — all without uploading anything to a cloud service.

### Brand Personality
**Empowering · Honest · Focused**

Bfree is a tool that respects its users. It's direct, transparent about what it does with data (nothing leaves the browser), and stays out of the way so riders can focus on their effort. The voice is confident but never corporate — open source, built by cyclists for cyclists.

**Emotional goal:** Empowered freedom — "I own my data, I'm in control, and this tool helps me ride better."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Sporty & bold** — high contrast, dynamic, athletic feel.

- **References:** Strava — clean but energetic, clear data hierarchy, feels alive and motivating. Strong use of brand color, athletic photography, bold typography for key metrics.
- **Anti-references:** Garmin Connect — too dense, cluttered, utilitarian without personality.
- **Theme:** Light mode only (current). Background `#fafafa`.
- **Accent color:** Keep the current MUI blue (`#1976D2`) as the primary accent. Secondary/error use `red.A400` (`#ff1744`).
- **Metric colors (existing):** Heart rate `#ffaeae`, Power `#b1e67b`, Speed `#57baeb`.
- **Typography:** Roboto (300/400/500/700) — keep as primary typeface. Use bolder weights and larger sizes for key metrics to create a sporty, data-forward hierarchy.

### Design Principles

1. **Metrics first** — Key numbers (power, heart rate, speed, time) are the hero. Make them large, bold, and instantly readable at a glance from the bike.
2. **Earn trust through transparency** — No hidden data flows, no dark patterns. The UI should feel open and honest, reflecting the project's privacy-first values.
3. **Get out of the way** — Minimize chrome and navigation friction. A rider mid-session needs zero cognitive overhead. Every tap should feel purposeful.
4. **Bold, not busy** — Use contrast, color, and scale to create energy — but never clutter. White space is an athlete's ally; it keeps the interface focused.
5. **Accessible by default** — Meet WCAG AA. Ensure sufficient contrast ratios, readable text at arm's length, touch-friendly targets (48px+), and respect for reduced motion preferences.

### Tech Stack & Styling Notes
- **Framework:** Next.js (Pages Router), React 18, TypeScript
- **Component library:** MUI 5 with Emotion
- **Charts:** Recharts
- **Maps:** Leaflet / react-leaflet
- **Current styling:** Mixed — `sx` props, `styled()`, CSS Modules. No centralized design tokens yet.
- **PWA:** Service worker registered in production for offline support.
